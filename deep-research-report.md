# Highway-Capable Electric Scooters in India

**Ola S1 Pro+ (Gen 3)** – The current leader in performance, the Ola S1 Pro+ (5.2 kWh) boasts a top speed of **130 km/h** with an IDC-certified range of **320 km**【33†L982-L989】. Its 13 kW peak motor (5.5 kW nominal) lets it sprint 0–40 km/h in ~2.1 s【33†L982-L989】. Even the standard S1 Pro (4.0 kWh) reaches **125 km/h** (242 km range)【33†L982-L989】. Features include dual 12″ disc brakes with ABS, regenerative braking, multi-mode traction control, cruise control and a 7″ touchscreen with maps and phone integration. Charging 0–80% takes about 7 hours with the on-board charger【33†L898-L904】.

**Ather 450X** – Ather’s flagship scooter is built like a lightweight motorcycle and is highway capable. It uses a 6.4 kW motor (26 Nm) and will top out at **90 km/h**【31†L318-L326】. With its 3.7 kWh battery pack and efficient drive, the 450X’s IDC range is about **161 km**【31†L324-L326】 (real-world around 100–120 km at sustained speeds). The ride is firm but poised; the Gen 4 450X accelerates 0–40 km/h in 3.3 s【31†L318-L326】. Safety features include dual 240 mm disc brakes with ABS, traction control, and FallSafe motor-cutoff. The 450X supports fast charging (around 30 km per 10 min on a 3.7 kW charger). An ultra-responsive TFT dashboard connects to smartphones.

**Simple OneS/One (Long-Range)** – Simple Energy’s scooters push the envelope for performance. The *Simple OneS* (3.7 kWh) achieves **90 km/h** top speed【19†L118-L120】 and about **190 km** (IDC) range on a full charge. Its punchy 6.4 kW motor (52 Nm) does 0–40 km/h in ~3.0 s【19†L118-L120】. The *Simple One (long-range variant)* ups the game: with a 5.0 kWh pack, it hits **115 km/h**【19†L128-L132】 and ~236 km range【19†L122-L126】, sprinting 0–40 in just 2.55 s【19†L128-L132】. Both models have dual-disc brakes with ABS, traction control and four levels of regenerative braking. (The even larger *Simple Ultra* with a 6.5 kWh pack is due soon, promising 115 km/h and 400 km range【19†L95-L103】【19†L106-L110】.)  

**Ampere Nexus** – Ampere’s new Nexus is a 3.0 kWh LFP-battery scooter aimed at performance. It is officially rated for **93 km/h** top speed【43†L69-L74】 with about **136 km** claimed range【18†L130-L133】 (realistic ~100–110 km at high speed). A 4 kW motor provides brisk acceleration and gradeability. Charging from 0–100% takes roughly 3.3 hours【18†L130-L133】. Nexus features a front disc brake (rear drum), high-brightness LED lights, a 7″ TFT display (on higher ST variant) and even a built-in GPS tracker. It’s the fastest in its sub-₹1.2 lakh segment【18†L125-L133】.

**TVS iQube ST (5.3 kWh)** – TVS’s iQube range tops out with the ST version. Its 5.3 kWh battery and 4.4 kW motor enable a top speed of about **82 km/h**【15†L585-L593】. IDC range is 212 km (real-world ~150 km) on a charge【16†L289-L298】. Braking is excellent with twin 220 mm discs (with combined-ABS), and the scooter has telescopic forks and twin rear shocks for stability. It also has an IP67 battery, regenerative braking, and a Bluetooth app. The ST variant 0–80% charges in ~4.2 hours【16†L290-L298】.  

**Bajaj Chetak Premium** – Bajaj’s Chetak is a premium city scooter with limited highway ability. Its 3.2 kWh battery (in Premium trim) delivers an IDC range of **126 km**【7†L142-L145】 and a top speed of **73 km/h**【7†L100-L104】 (the Urbane trim is slower). It’s more luxury-oriented, with a steel body, leatherette seat, LED lighting and underseat storage. The Chetak has front disc brake with CBS and a connected TFT display, but its speed limits keep it mainly in the city/highway periphery. A full charge takes ~4 hours【4†L106-L114】. (For true highways, its speed is on the low side.)

**Others:** A few other scooters merit mention: the **PURE EV EPluto 7G Max** can do ~72 km/h (with a 2.9–3.3 kWh battery, 150–211 km range【24†L182-L190】), and the **Komaki XR7** has very long range (322 km) but only ~55 km/h top speed. The **TVS X** is a large maxi-scooter with ~90 km/h speed (5 kWh, 150+ km range). Lower-speed commuters (Hero Electric models, most Chinese e-scooters, etc.) top out at 50–60 km/h and are not suitable for sustained highway use.

## Safety & Features Comparison

Most high-end e-scooters now come with robust safety kit. **Brakes:** Nearly all top models (Ola S1, Ather 450X, Simple One, TVS ST) use *dual disc brakes* (usually with dual-channel ABS or combined braking) for strong stopping power. The Ola S1 and Ather 450X explicitly list dual discs and ABS【33†L1018-L1022】【31†L179-L181】. (Ampere Nexus uses front disc/rear drum with CBS.) **Traction Control/HHC:** The Ola S1 Pro+ adds multi-mode traction control and hill-hold, and Ather 450X includes traction control and its proprietary “Traction Control” modes【31†L168-L175】【31†L179-L187】. Regenerative braking and single-mode KERS are standard too. **Build & Compliance:** All these scooters meet Indian safety regs (dual headlights, reflectors, mandatory horn, etc.). Many have IP-rated batteries (iQube is IP67) and theft alerts via apps. **Convenience:** Connectivity is universal: smartphone apps for navigation, GPS-tracking, remote lock, plus built-in TFT displays. Extras like cruise control, reverse assist, and voice activation (Ola’s “Voice” feature) are differentiators on Ola and Ather.

## Expert Feedback and Long-Term Use

Test reports confirm that these high-speed scooters handle highways adequately when driven smoothly. For example, reviewers note the Ather 450X and Simple OneS feel stable above 60 km/h, though suspension is firm for highway bumps. The Ola S1 series is praised for its smooth high-speed cruising and extensive tech (map navigation, connected services). In contrast, modest-speed scooters like the Bajaj Chetak or entry-level models often struggle on freeways. Long-term owners (Ather, Ola forums) report consistent performance if batteries are maintained; highway riding does reduce achievable range (e.g. an Ola S1 Pro+ might see ~200 km highway range at 80 km/h). Charging infrastructure (home fast charger and growing public DC chargers) mitigates range anxiety. 

## Best Picks (Pros & Cons)

- **Ola S1 Pro+ (Gen 3)**: *Pro:* Unmatched speed (130 km/h) and long range【33†L982-L989】; rich feature set (dual-ABS, connected dash, cruise). *Con:* Heaviest & most expensive.  
- **Ather 450X**: *Pro:* Refined handling, premium build, quick charging (30 km/10 min). *Con:* Range limited (~100 km real) and top speed only 90 km/h【31†L318-L326】.  
- **Simple One (Long-Range)**: *Pro:* Class-leading range and acceleration, 115 km/h top speed【19†L128-L132】. *Con:* Still a startup (service network smaller), slightly bulky.  
- **TVS iQube ST**: *Pro:* Rugged IP67 battery, excellent brakes, very good range for highway (≈150 km real). *Con:* Top speed (82 km/h) is lower than Ola/Ather; less tech integration.  
- **Ampere Nexus**: *Pro:* Affordable (~₹1.1 L), decent speed (93 km/h)【18†L130-L133】 and fast charging. *Con:* Mediocre real-world range (~100 km) and single disc rear brake.  
- **Bajaj Chetak Premium**: *Pro:* Solid build, comfortable ride and classic styling. *Con:* Low top speed (73 km/h)【7†L100-L104】 makes it unsuitable for sustained highway cruising.  
- **Honorable Mentions:** PURE EV EPluto 7G Max (72 km/h, long range) and Komaki XR7 (econobox with ~55 km/h) for long-distance touring at modest speed, though neither is highway-fast. 

**Price Range:** Expect highway-capable models to cost around ₹1.0–1.8 lakh (ex-showroom). For example, Ola S1 Pro+ starts ~₹1.55 lakh (Delhi)【33†L982-L989】; Ather 450X ~₹1.21 lakh (Mumbai)【31†L306-L311】; Ampere Nexus ~₹1.10 lakh (ex-showroom)【18†L125-L133】. Factor in subsidies and local taxes. 

In summary, the **Ola S1 Pro+ Gen 3** stands out for top speed and range, while the **Ather 450X** and **TVS iQube ST** offer trusted reliability. The **Simple One (long-range)** is ideal for riders who prioritize acceleration and range. Each choice trades off speed, range, and cost, but all above meet Indian highway speeds (60+ km/h). Prospective buyers should weigh these specs alongside comfort, service network and charging access for their routes.
